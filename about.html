About Me

The person behind Money Manners Guide

Hey, I'm Marcus Webb

I'm a 34-year-old software developer who stumbled into financial education by accident. After moving from London to Austin, Texas in 2019, I quickly realized that money works differently here — not just currencies, but everything from tipping expectations to tax obligations to how banks operate.

My first year in the States was a series of expensive lessons. That embarrassing undertipping incident I mention on the homepage? That was just the beginning. I also got hit with unexpected tax penalties for freelance work, overdrafted my account because I didn't understand hold policies, and nearly violated cash deposit regulations without realizing it.

By the end of that year, I had a choice: keep fumbling through financial situations or actually learn how things work. I chose to learn. And then I started writing it all down because surely other people were just as confused as I was.

Why This Site Exists

Let me be clear about something: I am not a financial advisor, accountant, or lawyer. I have no formal training in finance. What I do have is a methodical approach to research (thanks, programming background), access to the same publicly available information everyone else has, and firsthand experience with the confusion that financial systems create for regular people.

Get In Touch

I genuinely enjoy hearing from readers. If you've found an error, have a question I haven't covered, or just want to share your own financial learning experience, drop me a message.

— Marcus
